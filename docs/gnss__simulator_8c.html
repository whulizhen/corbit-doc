Kaila M. Y. Coimbra, Marta Cortinovis, Tara Mina and Grace Gao, Single-Satellite Lunar Navigation via Doppler Shift Observables for the NASA Endurance Mission, at ION2024 Delepaut et al. 2020, Misra and Enge,2012 C/N0 is a function of the received isometric power Pr, the receiver's gain-to-noise-temperature ratio g/T, k is the boltzmann constant (k=-228.8 dBW/KHz). C/N0 = Pr + g/T - k; Pr = EIRP(beta) - 20*log(10)(4*pi*r*Fs/c) ; beta is the off-boresight angle, Fs is the signal frequency, r is the distance between transmitter and receiver

g/T = Gr - Teq,
Gr is the gain of the receiver antenna in dB, could be 22.0 dB Teq = 10*log(10)(Tsys + 290*(10^(NFLNA/10) - 1)); the reference temperature at the input of the LNA is 290K. Tsys is the receiver's system noise temperature; could be 113K NFLNA is the noise figure from receiver's low noise amplifier(LNA), could be 1 dB
